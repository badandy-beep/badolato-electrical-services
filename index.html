AC power lights 160,000 bulbs at the Chicago World's Fair ‚Äî proving AC's superiority over Edison's DC.

1895

Tesla's AC system harnesses Niagara Falls, transmitting power to Buffalo, NY ‚Äî the first major AC power project.

1898

üõ°Ô∏è Electrical Safety Tips

Essential safety information for homeowners and renters.

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs to Watch

  • Circuit breakers that frequently trip
  • Flickering or dimming lights
  • Burning smell from outlets or switches
  • Discolored or warm outlet covers
  • Sparks when plugging in devices
  • Buzzing sounds from electrical panel
  • Mild shocks when touching appliances

If you notice any of these, call a licensed electrician immediately.

üè† Home Safety Basics

  • Test GFCI outlets monthly (push the TEST button)
  • Never overload outlets or power strips
  • Keep electrical devices away from water
  • Use proper wattage bulbs for fixtures
  • Don't run cords under rugs or furniture
  • Replace damaged or frayed cords immediately
  • Install tamper-resistant outlets for child safety

üåÄ Hurricane Season Prep

  • Know where your main electrical panel is located
  • Learn how to safely shut off power if needed
  • Never run generators indoors or in garages
  • Don't touch downed power lines ‚Äî assume they're live
  • Have flashlights with fresh batteries ready
  • Consider whole-home surge protection
  • Have generator safely installed by a licensed electrician

üîå Extension Cord Safety

  • Use extension cords only temporarily
  • Match cord rating to device power needs
  • Never daisy-chain multiple cords
  • Don't run cords through walls or ceilings
  • Unplug cords by pulling the plug, not the cord
  • Check for UL or CSA certification
